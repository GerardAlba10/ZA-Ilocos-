Festivals

Bac-Bacarra Festival

Held in Bacarra, Ilocos Norte, this festival honors the town’s history and showcases cultural dances, agricultural abundance, and local traditions through street parades and performances.

Guling-Guling Festival

This pre-Lenten festival in Paoay, Ilocos Norte celebrates tradition, faith, and forgiveness. People receive a cross marked with rice flour on their foreheads as a blessing.

Pamulinawen Festival

Celebrated in Laoag City, this festival pays tribute to the city’s patron saint, St. William the Hermit, through vibrant parades and street dancing inspired by Ilocano heritage.

Garlic Festival

Held in the town of Sinait, this festival promotes Ilocos’ famous garlic industry with cooking competitions, exhibits, and folk dances to honor the region’s “white gold.”

Empanada Festival

This festival in Batac City showcases the iconic Ilocos empanada with food fairs, cooking contests, and colorful parades to celebrate culinary creativity and community pride.

Dinaklisan Festival

Held in Currimao, Ilocos Norte, this coastal festival highlights the town’s fishing heritage through fluvial parades, seafood feasts, and traditional dances by the sea.

Folklore

Batibat / Bangungot

The Batibat is a vengeful, obese demon said to cause “bangungot” or sudden death during sleep. It’s believed to sit on a person’s chest and suffocate them during nightmares.

Lampong, The Guardian Anito

Lampong is a protective anito (spirit) in Ilocano lore who watches over families and households. It is often honored through offerings and prayers for good fortune and protection.

Litao, The Husband of Sirena

Litao is a mythical freshwater being in Filipino folklore believed to be the husband of the Sirena (mermaid). He dwells in rivers and lakes and can lure or protect humans depending on his mood.

Mangmangkik, The Tree Spirits

In Ilocano beliefs, Mangmangkik are spirits that inhabit trees, especially balete and acacia. Disturbing their dwellings can bring illness or misfortune unless rituals are performed.

The Monkey & The Turtle

This well-known Filipino folktale teaches a moral lesson about intelligence versus trickery. It follows a clever turtle who outsmarts a greedy monkey in a series of humorous encounters.

Pugot, The Headless Ghoul

The Pugot is a fearsome, headless creature in Ilocano folklore that often haunts dark places like abandoned houses or forests. Despite its terrifying appearance, it's sometimes considered harmless.
