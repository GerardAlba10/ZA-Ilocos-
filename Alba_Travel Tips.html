Tips for Travel Date, Transport & Locals Interaction

When to Travel
  • Off Season

    November - May : Due to Wet and Dry climates

  • Cool Season

    December - February : Cold Temperature

  • Peak Season

    April - May : Summer
    • How to Travel
    • By Bus

      Buses are a common way to reach the region from Manila. Major bus lines include Dominion Transit, Partas, Victory Liner, and Five Star. Dominion and Partas cover more northern destinations, while Victory and Five Star mostly serve Pangasinan.

    • By Vehicle

      You can drive via the Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEx) or the free alternative, Route 2. Other scenic routes include the Maharlika Highway near Pagudpud and Rte 114 through the Caraballo Mountains. Several roads also connect Ilocos to the Cordilleras.

    • By Plane

      Laoag International Airport (LAO) is the main airport with regular commercial flights. Smaller airports in Lingayen, San Fernando, and Vigan are used for charter flights or pilot training.
    Language of Locals
  • Local Language

    Locals speak Ilokano and Pangasinan (also known as Pangalatok, which is a derogatory term). Other languages include Igorot languages like Ibaloi in Pangasinan and Itneg (Tingguian) in Abra, as well as Sambal in the southwest and Bolinao in the west of the island. Residents of communities around the Nueva Ecija border speak Tagalog as their first language, and it is also spoken as a second language in other parts of the region.

  • English Fluency

    Due to their superior ability to enunciate English-specific sounds that are absent from most other main Philippine languages, such as Tagalog, Ilocanos are frequently seen by the majority of Filipinos as having superior English competence among the major Filipino ethnic groups. This view might also be influenced by the fact that Ilocanos make up the majority of the Filipino immigrant populations in the United States.
