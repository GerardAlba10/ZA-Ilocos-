Places to visit

Calle Crisologo (Vigan)

Famous for its well-preserved Spanish colonial and Asian architecture, Calle Crisologo offers a nostalgic walk through cobblestone streets, antique houses, and heritage shops in Vigan, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Cape Bojeador Lighthouse

Located in Burgos, Ilocos Norte, this century-old lighthouse sits on a hill overlooking the West Philippine Sea, guiding ships and offering panoramic coastal views.

Bantay Bell Tower

Standing beside the St. Augustine Church in Bantay, Ilocos Sur, this historic bell tower served as a watchtower during the Spanish colonial period.

Minor Basilica of Our Lady of The Rosary of Manaoag

Located in Pangasinan, this revered Catholic basilica attracts pilgrims year-round who come to venerate the miraculous image of Our Lady of Manaoag.

Saud Beach

Located in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, Saud Beach is known for its white sand shores and turquoise waters — a peaceful tropical getaway ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and sunset watching.

Bangui Windmills

Stretching along the Bangui Bay in Ilocos Norte, these massive wind turbines are part of the Philippines' first wind farm and symbolize sustainable energy in the region.

Ma-Cho Temple

Located in La Union, this Taoist temple is dedicated to the Chinese sea goddess Mazu and features traditional architecture with panoramic views of the city and ocean.

Pagburnayan Jar Factory

Experience traditional pottery-making in Vigan at this heritage site where artisans shape clay jars (burnay) using ancient techniques passed down through generations.

Blue Lagoon Beach

Nestled in Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte, Blue Lagoon is known for its crystal-clear waters, fine white sand, and dramatic coastal cliffs — perfect for swimming and relaxation.

Paoay Church

Also known as the St. Augustine Church, this 18th-century baroque church is famous for its massive buttresses and has withstood multiple earthquakes over the centuries.

Sand Dunes (Laoag City)

The La Paz Sand Dunes offer a unique desert-like landscape where tourists can enjoy thrilling 4x4 rides and sandboarding amidst rolling golden hills.

Kapurpurawan Rock Formation

Carved by wind and sea, this stunning white limestone rock formation along the Burgos coast is a natural marvel and a favorite spot for photography and sightseeing.
