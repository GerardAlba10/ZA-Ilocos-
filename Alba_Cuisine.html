Food for your cravings

Vigan Longganisa

Vigan Longganisa is a garlicky and slightly sour Filipino sausage native to Vigan. It’s often served fried and paired with rice and eggs for a hearty breakfast.

Ilocos Empanada

This deep-fried snack is made with a rice flour dough filled with grated green papaya, mung bean sprouts, longganisa, and egg, wrapped in orange-colored dough.

Bagnet

Bagnet is a crispy pork belly dish from Ilocos that’s deep-fried to golden perfection. It's similar to lechon kawali but known for an even crunchier skin.

Pinakbet

Pinakbet is a savory vegetable dish made with squash, bitter melon, eggplant, okra, and string beans, flavored with bagoong (fermented fish sauce).

Okoy Tiyosko

Okoy is a Filipino shrimp fritter made with glutinous rice, mung bean sprouts, and sometimes grated vegetables, deep-fried to a crispy golden brown.

Poqui Poqui

Poqui Poqui is a soft and savory Ilocano eggplant dish sautéed with garlic, onions, tomatoes, and beaten eggs — often eaten with rice.

Dudol

Dudol (or dudul) is a native rice cake made with glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and brown sugar — thick, sticky, and traditionally served during fiestas.

Chichacorn

Chichacorn is a crunchy corn snack from Ilocos that’s deep-fried and comes in various flavors like cheese, garlic, and barbecue — perfect for munching.

Tupig

Tupig is a grilled rice cake made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and brown sugar, wrapped in banana leaves and cooked over charcoal.
